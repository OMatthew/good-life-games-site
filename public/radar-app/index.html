How parcels are selected and ranked

The app first keeps parcels that match the basic acreage, category, and coverage rules, then ranks them by access, flood position, market signals, price, district signals, and ownership.

Qualify parcels first

A parcel can be ranked when it is at least 5 acres, is categorized as commercial or land, and represents one distinct site. If county data splits one site across several accounts, the app shows one representative account. Confirmed parcels are vacant or have building footprint coverage below 10%. Needs confirmation means value or building records suggest improvements, but footprint data is missing or unclear, so coverage needs manual review.

Score formula

Score = owner warning multiplier * 100 * (20% road access + 20% flood position + 20% area momentum + 20% relative price + 10% serviceability proxy + 10% ownership)

Each part scores from 0 to 1. Missing inputs score neutral at 0.5. Final scores are capped at 0-100 and rounded to one decimal. The owner warning multiplier is 1.00 for ordinary owners and 0.65 for likely-unbuyable owner classes.

What the subscores mean

Road access: closer to a TxDOT major corridor scores better. Frontage = 1.00, quarter mile = 0.75, mile = 0.45, far = 0.15, missing = 0.50.
Flood position: outside mapped zones = 1.00, 500-year = 0.70, 100-year = 0.35, floodway = 0.00 plus manual-review badge.
Area momentum: neighborhood value growth and recent permit activity. Stronger local movement scores higher.
Relative price: lower appraised land value per acre scores higher versus nearby peers when enough peers exist, otherwise versus the county.
Serviceability proxy: city = 1.00, MUD = 0.80, special district = 0.60, no district = 0.40, missing = 0.50. This is a district-membership signal, not proof of utilities, taps, detention, or entitlements.
Ownership: more years since the HCAD owner-change date and absentee owners score higher. Absentee is based on mailing ZIP differing from site ZIP.

Visible warnings

Floodway parcels and likely-unbuyable owner classes stay visible. They rank lower or carry warning flags instead of being silently excluded. Likely-unbuyable classes include HOA/POA, cemetery, church, pipeline/utility, and government/remnant owners.
